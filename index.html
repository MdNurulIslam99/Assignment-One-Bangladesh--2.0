Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh has shown remarkable innovation in sectors like microfinance, renewable energy, and ICT, initiatives Grameen Bank and Solar Home Systems

Sustainability

Bangladesh's sustainability faces challenges due to climate change, overpopulation, and resource constraints, but efforts in renewable energy disaster resilience.

Infrastructure

The infrastructure of Bangladesh is rapidly developing, with significant investments in transportation, energy, and urbanization projects like the Padma Bridge and metro rail systems.

Empowerment

The empowerment of Bangladesh lies in its strides toward gender equality, education, and economic growth. Through innovation and resilience, the nation continues to shape a brighter future.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a renowned Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, banker, and economist, best known for his pioneering work in microfinance. He is the founder of Grameen Bank, which provides microloans to the impoverished in Bangladesh, particularly women, to help them start small businesses and escape the cycle of poverty. His work has earned him international recognition, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006, which he shared with Grameen Bank for their efforts in creating economic and social development from below.
